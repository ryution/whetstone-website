The New Standard

Strong Scores Won't Open Doors. But They Certainly Will Close Them.

In New York and other competitive districts, strong SAT & ACT scores are table stakes. A 1500 SAT and a 34 ACT is no longer exceptional.

That is where most test prep falls short. Students are not motivated to practice and quickly forget material between lessons. Group classes are often too large for real accountability, while one-to-one tutoring can be inefficient and draining.

Whetstone was built for this. Every student is taught in small cohorts of motivated students by tutors who've scored in the 99th percentile. We set a target and don't stop until the student succeeds.

Our Method

How Whetstone Students Actually Improve

Repetition alone is not enough. Improvement comes from mastering the fundamentals, then applying them under pressure until strong performance becomes repeatable.

1

Active Recall

Students are trained to retrieve and restate ideas from memory instead of relying on open notes. Research has found gains of over 50% in long-term retention compared with passive review.

2

Feynman Method

Physicist and Nobel laureate Richard Feynman became known for learning until he could explain ideas simply enough to teach them to a child. Students work in pairs using this method to turn errors into deeper understanding.

3

Heavy Testing

Students train under strict timing, working up to practice with up to 30% less time. The goal is to build calm, speed, and precision before test day.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

When should my student start tutoring? +
Most students begin in summer of their sophomore year to prepare for August, September, or October exams. We recommend starting with a diagnostic early to allow the most time for improvement to base skills like reading prior to the test prep intensive.
Should my child take the SAT or the ACT? +
Both tests are accepted equally by every major college and university. The right choice depends on the student's strengths. We administer a diagnostic for both and recommend the test where the student has a larger natural advantage.
How is the Digital SAT different from the old paper test? +
The Digital SAT (launched March 2024) is shorter (2 hours 14 minutes vs. 3 hours), adaptive by module, and delivered on a computer or tablet. The adaptive format means early performance heavily influences your final score. Our tutors are trained specifically on Digital SAT strategy and official College Board practice materials.
How are Whetstone instructors selected? +
Our instructors are exclusively recruited from those who've scored in the top 99th percentile on exams they teach before working with students.
How does the score guarantee work? +
Each program tier includes a specific score guarantee. If your student puts reasonable effort behind the program (attending 85% of sessions and completing 85% of assigned practice) and does not hit the target score we set together, we will keep working for free until we succeed. Details are outlined in the enrollment agreement.
